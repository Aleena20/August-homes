Why we built this

Hosts told us exactly
what's broken.

We interviewed 25 short-term rental hosts across nine European cities before writing a line of product copy. This is what came back.

40%

Guests forget the code

The single largest source of failed check-ins. Codes get lost in inboxes, mistyped, or simply forgotten by the time a guest is standing at the door.

28%

Already own a smart lock

Hardware was never the gap. Hosts who already use a smart lock still report the same coordination pain — because nothing talks to their booking calendar.

80%

Lose 20+ minutes per booking

Across every city we visited, hosts described the same pattern: messaging guests, updating lockbox codes, chasing cleaners — every single booking, by hand.

How it works

Three things happen.
You do none of them.

01

A booking is confirmed

The moment a guest books on Airbnb or Booking.com, August Home reads the reservation dates directly from your calendar.

📅
02

A key is generated and sent

A time-limited key is created automatically and delivered by SMS or web link — no app download, no account creation required for your guest.

🔑
03

Access expires at checkout

The key revokes itself the moment the stay ends. The event is written to your compliance log automatically — nothing for you to track.

🔒

Built for European regulation

Compliance isn't a footnote.
It's a feature.

Vienna caps rentals at 90 nights a year. Barcelona at 30. Every host we spoke to in a regulated city said the same thing: tracking it manually is its own job.

01

GDPR by design

No guest account required. Access data is minimal, encrypted, and never sold or shared with third parties.

02

Night-cap tracking

Every check-in counts automatically toward your local rental-night limit — visible on your dashboard in real time.

03

Exportable evidence

A single PDF, ready for any local authority request, generated in seconds — not assembled from memory.

04

Consent-first guests

Guests grant access without creating an account or sharing biometric data — eliminating the refusals hosts told us they were seeing.
